Ares Capital Corporation (ARCC) reported Q4 2025 earnings that demonstrated continued resilience across its $22B+ portfolio. Core EPS of $0.55 beat consensus by $0.02, supported by strong net investment income and stable credit quality across the book.

## Portfolio Quality

Non-accrual investments declined to 1.2% of portfolio fair value (vs. 1.5% in Q3 2025), reflecting active portfolio management and the broader improvement in the leveraged credit environment. The weighted average yield on debt investments held steady at 11.4%.

## Dividend Coverage and Sustainability

ARCC generated NII of $0.55 per share against a quarterly dividend of $0.48, producing a 114% coverage ratio — the fourth consecutive quarter of coverage above 110%. Management declared an unchanged $0.48/share regular dividend for Q1 2026, plus a $0.03 supplemental distribution.

The 12-month trailing dividend yield at current price stands at 8.8%, with supplementals adding approximately 60bps annually.

## Valuation Update

At $21.85, ARCC trades at 1.13x NAV ($19.27), a modest premium that we view as justified given its:
- Scale ($22B+ AUM) and resulting access to deal flow
- Diversification (450+ portfolio companies, 25+ industries)
- Conservative underwriting track record across credit cycles
- AAA/AA credit facility access at favorable spreads

Our base case of $23.50 represents 1.22x forward NAV, in line with historical premium ranges during periods of strong NII coverage.
